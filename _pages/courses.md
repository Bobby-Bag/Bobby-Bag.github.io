# Academic Coursework

**Institution:** University of Houston-Clear Lake  
**Degrees:** A.S. Computer Science | B.S. Computer Information Systems *(Cum Laude, Dec 2024)* | M.S. Data Science *(In Progress)*  

---

## Graduate Coursework — M.S. Data Science

### Spring 2026

**DASC 5231 — Data Visualization**  
Focuses on the principles and tools used to communicate data insights through visual representation. Covers design theory, storytelling with data, and practical use of visualization libraries and dashboarding tools.

**DASC 5431 — Data Analytics & Machine Learning**  
An applied study of core machine learning algorithms and analytical techniques. Topics span supervised and unsupervised learning, model evaluation, feature engineering, and the deployment of predictive models on real datasets.

**DASC 5931 — Research Topics in Data Science**  
A seminar-style course exploring current and emerging research areas within **natural language processing**. Students engage with recent literature, identify open problems, and develop skills in framing and communicating original research contributions. 

---

### Fall 2025

**CINF 5432 — Data Warehousing & Business Intelligence**  
Examines the architecture and design of data warehouses and the business intelligence systems built on top of them. Topics include ETL pipelines, dimensional modeling, OLAP, and data-driven decision-making frameworks.

**DASC 5131 — Data Programming in Python**  
A hands-on introduction to Python as the primary tool for data science workflows. Covers core libraries such as NumPy, Pandas, and Matplotlib, with a focus on data wrangling, exploration, and automation of analytical tasks.

**DASC 5133 — Introduction to Data Science**  
Provides a comprehensive overview of the data science lifecycle, from data acquisition and cleaning to exploratory analysis, modeling, and communication of results. Establishes foundational concepts in machine learning and statistical thinking within a data science context.

---

### Summer 2025

**CSCI 5737 — Mobile Applications Development**  
An exploration of the design and development of applications for iOS platforms. Topics include Swift programming, mobile UI/UX principles, platform-specific frameworks, and deploying functional apps to modern mobile ecosystems.

**STAT 5135 — Applied Statistical Methods**  
A graduate-level treatment of statistical methods with emphasis on practical application. Covers foundational inference, regression modeling, hypothesis testing, and the use of statistical software to analyze real-world datasets.

## Undergraduate Coursework — B.S. Computer Information Systems

### Fall 2024

**CINF 4364 — Computer Systems Administration**  
Covers the administration and management of computing infrastructure, including operating system configuration, user management, system monitoring, and enterprise-level IT operations and maintenance. *Emphasis on UNIX systems administration*

**ITEC 4342 — IT Project Management**  
Introduces project management methodologies as applied to technology initiatives. Topics include scope definition, scheduling, budgeting, risk management, and team coordination using industry-standard frameworks such as PMP and Agile.

**MATH 2305 — Discrete Mathematics**  
Covers the mathematical structures most relevant to computing, including logic, set theory, combinatorics, graph theory, and proof techniques foundational to algorithm design and theoretical computer science.

**CINF 4388 — Senior Project in Computer Information Systems**  
A capstone experience in which students apply knowledge and skills accumulated throughout the CIS program to a substantial, independently managed project addressing a real-world information systems problem.

---

### Spring 2024

**CINF 4324 — Modern Systems Analysis and Design**  
Covers the methodologies and tools used to analyze organizational information needs and design effective system solutions. Topics include requirements elicitation, process modeling, and both agile and traditional SDLC approaches.

**CSCI 4320 — Web Application Development**  
A practical course in building dynamic, full-stack web applications primarily with ASP.NET. Covers front-end and back-end development technologies, RESTful API design, and the deployment of web-based software solutions.

**ITEC 4383 — Cyber Security II**  
Builds on foundational security knowledge with advanced topics including penetration testing, security auditing, intrusion detection systems, digital forensics, and policy frameworks for enterprise security governance.

**SWEN 4342 — Software Engineering**  
Examines the engineering principles behind large-scale software development, including project planning, requirements analysis, software architecture, testing strategies, and quality assurance practices.

---

### Fall 2023

**CINF 3331 — Business Data Communications**  
Covers the fundamentals of data communications and networking as they apply to business environments. Topics include transmission media, network architectures, protocols, and the infrastructure underlying enterprise connectivity.

**CINF 4321 — Enterprise Resource Planning Systems**  
Examines integrated enterprise software solutions that unify business functions such as finance, supply chain, and HR. Students gain exposure to ERP configuration, implementation challenges, and real-world organizational use cases.

**LEGL 3301 — Business Law**  
Introduces the legal framework governing business operations, including contract law, tort liability, business entity structures, intellectual property, and regulatory compliance in commercial contexts.

**MGMT 3301 — Management Theory and Practice**  
Surveys classical and contemporary management theories, exploring how organizations plan, organize, lead, and control resources to achieve strategic objectives.

**MKTG 3301 — Principles of Marketing**  
Provides an overview of core marketing concepts including market research, consumer behavior, product development, pricing strategy, distribution channels, and promotional communications.

---

### Summer 2023

**CSCI 4323 — Computer Security**  
A deeper technical examination of computer and network security concepts. Covers attack methodologies, defense strategies, secure coding practices, and tools used in vulnerability assessment and incident response. *Heavy focus in cryptology*

**CSCI 4333 — Design of Database Systems**  
Explores the theory and practice of designing relational database systems. Topics include the entity-relationship model, normalization, SQL, transaction management, and performance considerations for large-scale data stores.

---

### Spring 2023

**CINF 3321 — Information Systems Theory & Practice**  
Bridges theoretical frameworks and hands-on application in information systems. Covers systems thinking, organizational IS strategy, and the interplay between business processes and technology solutions.

**DSCI 3331 — Quantitative Methods for Management**  
Applies quantitative analytical techniques to managerial decision-making. Topics include linear programming, decision analysis, forecasting, and optimization methods used in business environments.

**ITEC 3388 — Cyber Security I**  
An introduction to the principles of cybersecurity, including threat landscapes, network vulnerabilities, access control, cryptography basics, and best practices for securing information systems.

**WRIT 3312 — Written Communications in Business**  
Focuses on effective written communication within professional and business contexts, including reports, memos, emails, and persuasive documents tailored to organizational audiences.

---

### Fall 2022

**CINF 1370 — Introduction to Computer Information Systems**  
A survey of the foundational concepts underlying computer information systems, including hardware, software, data management, networking, and the role of technology in modern organizations.

**STAT 3308 — Computational Statistics**  
Introduces statistical reasoning and computation, with a focus on using software tools to perform data analysis. Topics include descriptive statistics, probability distributions, and introductory inferential methods.

---

### Spring 2022

**WRIT 3315 — Advanced Technical Writing**  
Develops advanced skills in producing clear, concise, and audience-appropriate technical documentation. Covers reports, proposals, manuals, and professional correspondence within technology-focused contexts.
